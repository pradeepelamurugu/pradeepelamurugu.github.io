Agriculture - The heart of future India

The growing population poses a challenge to food and nutritional securities when there is pressure on land due to urbanisation, improved standard of living, changed food habits etc, generating great demand for diversified agricultural commodities. These challenges are further exacerbated by declining soil health, low nutrient content, the occurrence of new biotic stresses and frequent droughts and floods due to climate change.

Food quality and safety are the two important factors that have gained ever-increasing attention in general consumers. The foods we intake nowadays are becoming highly toxic. Hence practising organic farming may cover these problems. But a major concern is that even though it is highly applicable for vegetables and fruit crops, it is difficult for field crops (since they are grown in big areas). Comment your opinions on the above situation. Is there any possibility of converting whole farming practices in India to organic? Share your field of vision regarding this.
Agriculture plays a very crucial role in AtmaNirbhar Bharat. This mission focuses on the importance of promoting local products. This mission is also expected to complement the (Make in India) initiative which intends to encourage manufacturing in India including the agriculture sector which has great potential for the same.
As like every other sector agriculture also produces tonnes and tonnes of residues. This poses a great challenge in managing them as well. Starting from crop residues to post-harvest wastage, an integrated and standard way of management strategy is in need. A comprehensive approach for the same is most welcome.

Natural Resource Management

Natural resource management refers to the sustainable utilization of major natural resources such as land, water, air, minerals, forests, fisheries, wild flora and fauna. Together these resources provide ecosystem services that improve the quality of human life.

Precision farming is an approach where inputs are utilized in precise amounts to get increased average yields, compared to traditional cultivation techniques. In India, one major problem regarding precision agriculture is the small field size. How can we help the small farmers today to take up precision farming?
Radio collaring/ radio telemetry is a technique for determining bird movements over areas ranging in size from the restricted breeding territories to the movement patterns of international migratory species. To the efficacy study of the technique, worldwide outbreaks could also be studied.
Tropical forests harbouring rich biodiversity are responding in several ways to global climate change leading to shifts in species composition and an overall increase in turnover. Forest vegetation contains over 35000 Tg of carbon, of which tropical forests accounts for 40% and play a major role in the global carbon cycle. Estimation of above-ground biomass is an essential aspect of carbon sequestration studies since it constitutes about 60% of the total phytomass. Due to the lack of reliable data on standing biomass and rates of forest degradation, the hot carbon emission estimates for India are highly variable. Thus the improved quantification of carbon pools and fluxes in forest ecosystems is important for understanding the contribution of forests to the net carbon emissions and their potential for carbon sequestration.

Horticulture

Horticulture is the agriculture of plants, mainly for food, materials, comfort and beauty for decoration. Horticulturists apply knowledge, skills, and technologies to grow intensively produced plants for human food and non-food uses and for personal or social needs.

Urban horticulture is the specific study of the relationship between plants and the urban environment. It focuses on the functional use of horticulture to maintain and improve the surrounding urban area. Nowadays, the lands are converted into urban areas. So how will we utilize the reduced land space to attain daily needs?
Innovative value addition of fruits, vegetables, spices, plantation crops and floricultural crops are getting momentum nowadays, this is a field which has a creativity calling and more scope for imagination than calculated, routine work. Innovation is an important factor in the growth and development of the horticultural sector both in India and around the world. So bring and share your innovations for more development and growth.
India wastes more fruits and vegetables than it consumes. About 30% of fruits and vegetables grown in India (40 m tons) amounting to 13 billion US dollars get wasted annually due to gaps in the cold chain. So propose a viable solution to minimise the post-harvest losses.

Crop management

The goal of crop production management is to produce more with less, which can be achieved by maximising efficiency. This will boost the profitability of any farm’s operations and allows further development. The real beauty of crop management lies in the fact that it not only optimises operations but does so while preserving the environment and natural resources.

One of the major challenges to humankind is the threat to food security due to emerging and invasive pests. Increased global trade in agriculture has increased the chances of introduction to exotic pests. So what are the steps to be undertaken to mitigate the effects of new exotic pests in India.
In natural systems, plants face a plethora of antagonists and thus possess a myriad of defence and have evolved multiple defence mechanisms by which they can cope with various kinds of pests and disease. Identification of new and viable metabolites from native plants and trees and their compounding application against pest and disease control is in much need. Emerging Secondary Metabolites pose a valuable alternative against traditional pesticides. Innovative thoughts are looked forward to!
Microbial communities and their multi-functionalities play a crucial role in the management of soil and plant health and thus they help in managing agro-ecology, the environment and agriculture. Give your ideas about pest, disease and natural calamities management using microorganisms.

Agricultural economics

Agricultural economics plays an important role in the economics of development for a continuous level of farm supplies is one of the wellsprings of technological & commercial growth. Contributions to the field of research are training, smallholder & rural development, project planning & management, and the formulation, planning & analysis of macro-policy.

Availability of fresh food products in the cities of India is still a dream, and the sustainable market opportunities and the premium price for the farm produce is still magic fruit. Bringing together these two negatives, a positive light of partnership between those would be a great boom. A strategic plan for this innovative idea is the needed talk. Such is awaited.
The Government of India in its annual Budget 2016 to 2017 set a policy target for farmers’ income by 2022 . Agriculture sustains livelihood for more than half of India’s total population. Doubling farmers’ income in a short period is an overwhelming task for decision-makers , scientists and policymakers because of its continued role in employment, income and most importantly in national food security. It is believed that doubling farmer’s income is possible through increasing total output and better price realisation in the market, reduction in production cost, diversification of products, efficient post-harvest management, value addition etc. Do you think it is possible? Give your ideas.
The future market is now emerging as a promising platform for protecting farmers from future price fluctuations through hedging. It is also an effective instrument for price discovery. The issue is to enable the small and marginal farmers to participate in this marketing system. Hence there is a need for the extension functionaries to aggregate the farmers at the grassroots level to enable them to participate in this market.
